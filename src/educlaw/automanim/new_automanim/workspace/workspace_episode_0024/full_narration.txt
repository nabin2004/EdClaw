This is a loss surface for a simple quadratic function: the loss equals weight squared. We start at weight w zero equals three, and compute the gradient. With a normal learning rate of zero point one, gradient descent converges smoothly to the minimum. Now let's see what happens with an exploding gradient, using a learning rate of one point five, which is much larger than the critical value. The overshoot is catastrophic! The weights flip to the opposite side with even larger magnitude. Each update drives the weights further away from the minimum, causing the system to diverge completely. A large learning rate causes weights to explode, making training impossible.
